A not-so-simple cheddar cheese recipe!

Ingredients

  • 2 Gallons of cow milk
  • 1/8 Tsp calcium chloride
  • Mesophilic culture
  • Rennet, dissolved into 1/2 cool water
  • 2 Tbsp sea salt

Steps

  1. In a large pot, heat the milk to 85°F, stirring frequently
  2. As the milk is heating, add calcium chloride.
  3. When milk reaches 85°F, add culture, and stir.
  4. Cover and ferment for 1 hour.
  5. Stir to homogenize the milk, and slowly fold in the diluted rennet.
  6. Remove from heat source and allow the cheese to set for 1 hour.
  7. Cut the curds into 1/4 inch cubes and allow them to set for 5 minutes.
  8. Over the next 30 minutes, slowly heat the curds to 100°F, stirring frequently.
  9. After 30 minutes, stop stirring and allow the curds to settle.
  10. Pour the curds into a colander. Place the curds back into the cheese pot and allow to drain for 15 minutes.
  11. Remove colander from the pot and turn the curds out onto a cutting board.
  12. Fill a sink or basin with 102°F water and place the pot and curds into it. turn the slices every 15 minutes for 2 hours.
  13. Remove curds from pot and cut into 1/2 inch cubes. Place back in pot, cover, and place in the water.
  14. Remove pot from sink and add salt. Line the cheese with a piece of cheese cloth, wrap the cloth around the cheese and press for 15 minutes.
  15. Remove the cheese from the press, unwrap, and flip the cheese. Press at 40 pounds of pressure for 12 hours.
  16. Remove the cheese from the press, unwrap, and flip the cheese. Press at 50 pounds of pressure for 24 hours.
  17. Remove the cheese from the press and air-dry for 2 to 3 days, until smooth and dry to the touch.
  18. Wax the cheese and age at 55° to 60°F for at least 60 days.
